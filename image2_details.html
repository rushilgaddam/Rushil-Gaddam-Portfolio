Project Explanation

In cancer, immune cells often infiltrate tumors, either to attack the cancer cells or, unfortunately, to become suppressed by the tumor's defensive mechanisms. Calculating cell density, which measures the number of specific cells within a given area, provides insights into the level and intensity of immune response in various regions of the tumor. For example, a higher density of CD8+ killer t-cells may indicate a strong immune response aimed at targeting cancer cells. These densities were compared among gender and deep or superficial tissue
